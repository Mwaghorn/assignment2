Industry Data vs SOMMI

Industry Data vs SOMMI Job Titles
Technology is a fast-growing industry, and as the industry grows so too do the types of roles that are required and the demand for those roles. In the Ideal Jobs section Mitchell noted that there were three roles that appeared as part of the group’s personal profiles, these include Software Developer which appeared three times; with one instance of Senior Software Developer amongst the three, and Full Stack Developer and Database Specialist which both appeared once each.

Surprisingly, this is in direct contradiction to with the Burning Glass findings where the data indicates that the Full Stack Developer role was the most in demand of the three roles with 344 jobs advertised, followed closely by the Software Developer role with 337 jobs advertised. Unfortunately, there is no statistic for Database Specialist, however, based on the information available it may be safe to extrapolate that the Burning Glass findings for a Database Specialist/Administrator role are in line with the group’s ideal roles.

Industry Data vs SOMMI Job Skills
By further analysing the roles listed by Burning Glass Technologies, it becomes apparent that many of the roles listed require at least one of two elements. The first is years of experience, this is apparent in cases such as the Senior .Net Developer role which; when looking at the first 10 roles on LinkedIn to mention tenure; required an average of 6.4 years of .Net experience. This figure, when compared with the release date of the ‘.Net Framework in 2002 (Tauqir, 2020)’ almost 19 years ago and its successor ‘.Net Core in 2016 (Microsoft, 2021)’ is quite high.

The second is the requirement for either broad knowledge or a specialised skill set. The former is demonstrated in the Service Desk Analyst role which requires various skills ranging from basic customer service to more broad skills such as a knowledge of ‘ITIL frameworks, Citrix and Service Now (Seek, n.d)’. On the other hand, roles such as the Php Developer role require more nuanced knowledge such as the knowledge of the ‘Laravel Framework and jQuery (seek, n.d).

Within the group, the necessary skills identified for the ideal roles were quite generic and range from ‘excellent problem-solving skills’ and the ‘ability to work independently and collaboratively’ which was a requirement for all three roles to ‘analytical and methodical thinking’ and ‘high attention to detail’ which appeared once each for the Software Developer role and Database Specialist role respectively. Whilst these skills ranked amongst the top twenty ‘in demand skills’ within the Burning Glass data, and it’s important for all the skills listed within the Burning Glass data to be present in a potential candidate; the top three skills that were not present within the group’s required skill set are: writing, troubleshooting and planning. This is surprising because the planning of solutions, prioritisation of work, troubleshooting and documentation of the above plays a prominent part in each of these roles. As a result, it is likely that these skills may have been omitted from the required skill list because they are aspects of tech roles that are often overlooked or thought of as necessary evils.

Another aspect that was overlooked within the group’s required skill set are the IT specific skills. As a result, the group’s findings could not be compared with the Burning Glass data however, by reviewing the data you can easily determine what three IT specific skills are important for each role and what skills are an overall requirement. For instance, the knowledge of SQL is an IT specific skill that is important to the Database Specialist role, whilst the knowledge of JavaScript and Java are more likely to be IT specific skills required for the Software Developer and Full Stack Developer roles. Further to this, the ability to use Microsoft Windows or Linux is a requirement for all three roles but is usually determined (either) by preference or company requirements.

Industry Data vs SOMMI Job Titles - Re-evaluated
Based on the Burning Glass data, the group’s ideal jobs have not changed. However, it is important to note that the Burning Glass data is almost three years old and a more recent study by Robert Half Technology seems to indicate that the Database Administrator role is more in demand, ranking third in the ‘top 10 most in-demand jobs for 2021 (White, 2020)’ than the Software Developer and Full Stack Developer roles which have been amalgamated and have fallen to eight within this list.
