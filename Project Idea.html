Scope and Limitations

Overview
SOMMI have decided to develop an application called Mealr. This application will be available as a mobile application; for both iOS and Android devices; and as a web application for larger devices The purpose of the application is to provide end users with a range of recipes that can be filtered based on dietary requirements and preferences.
In the future, the application will also provide end users with the ability to create a shopping cart that is linked directly to their preferred supermarket chain and location.

Initial Scope
The largest part of realising this project is the building of the application itself. However, due to fact that the team does not possess the required skills to build an application, the team have chosen to focus on developing and presenting the user interface and user experience. This allows the team to present the overall idea to a group of investors.
Within the time provided the team aims to refine the overall application’s aesthetic and build a shell for the web application based on the colour scheme chosen (see below). The consistency across both the web and mobile applications means that end users have a seamless experience regardless of their chosen device, or if the end user is switching between devices.

Future Features
As previously mentioned, the minimum viable product for Mealr is the overall ability to search for and upload recipes based on an end user’s dietary requirements. Future iterations of the application will feature the ability to create shopping carts that automatically scale ingredient requirements based on the number of servings required and will utilise vertical integration with supermarket chains such as Coles and Woolworths to provide end users with the ability to shop within the application.

Motivation and Point of Difference

We are very interested in Mealr as several of us have previously, or currently still do subscribe to meal kit services however, these meal kits are somewhat limited when it comes to dietary requirements for the recipes that are produced each week. The most popular meal kit services such as Hello Fresh, Dinnerly, and Marley Spoon do offer meals and ingredients that are vegetarian and contain no added gluten however there are not as many of these meals compared to the meals offered that contain meat or gluten (Marley Spoon, 2021). Further to this, many people do also have requirements of consuming only halal or kosher certified foods. The ingredients provided in these meal kits often don’t state whether they are halal or kosher certified and may cause apprehension for people with this requirement. Meal kit services are also not as readily available for people who do adhere to halal and kosher requirements. The only halal or kosher meal kits available in Melbourne are Halal Dinnerbox and Kosher Meals on Wheels, both of which only provide their customers with pre-packaged and precooked food, limiting the customer on being able to enjoy freshly cooked meals. If a user lives far away from a large city, then the likelihood of there being such service that can deliver to them is very slim.

The point of difference for Mealr is to allow users with very specific dietary requirements to enjoy the convenience of meal planning and not having to think about if the food they are making is okay to consume, while also enjoying their meals being as fresh as possible since they are not pre-packaged. Mealr also allows users to save the cost of delivery if they wish to go to their supermarket or local food store to purchase their ingredients. The price of delivery for meal kits such as Dinnerly, Marley Spoon, and Hello Fresh is $10.00 per box sent (Marley Spoon, 2021). If a user received a box each week, this is an additional $520.00 over the course of a year, which can easily be saved if the user wishes.

Supermarkets such as Coles and Woolworths also have a similar product on their website, which allows users to shop recipes and add the items required to their shopping list and predict the total cost of the meal. Mealr will differ to this by taking it an extra step further and ensure that the products that are a part of the recipe match the dietary requirement selected by the user. If a user is strictly vegan, then only products that are 100% vegan will be shown on their shopping list. This point of different will allow users of Mealr to not have to investigate the ingredient list of every item they buy, saving the user time and potential headaches.

How will it work?

Mealr will work by being a database that stores recipes that are released on a weekly basis. The recipes that will appear on Mealr are produced by chefs and by users that have an account and wish to submit recipes they would like to share. Each recipe that is sent in by a user will be analysed and considered to ensure that only quality ingredients are being sent in. The users of Mealr can browse the recipes that are released by applying filters based on the dietary requirements that apply to them. Multiple filters can also be selected at once. The filters will include the following: vegetarian, vegan, shellfish-free, dairy-free, nut-free, nut-free, gluten-free, halal, and kosher.

After the user selects the appropriate filter or filters, Mealr will then show the recipes that match the selected filter or filters. Each week there will be ten recipes for each filter that are released to ensure that all users of the app are given a variety of options to choose. It’s often a pain point that there are not enough options presented to people with specific dietary requirements in popular meal kits, so it’s important to provide an equal number of options for all users of the application.

Once the user has selected the recipes they wish to cook for that week, users will be asked how many portions they intend to prepare for each meal. If a user wishes to cook for a family of four, then four portions will be selected for each recipe. After this is established, users of Mealr will be asked what common household cooking ingredients they have, such as garlic, salt, sugar, oil, eggs, and soy sauce. The reason for this is to remove these items from the user’s generated shopping list so when it is produced, they only need to buy the ingredients that they don’t have. Once this has been done, the user will be presented with their shopping list for the week, and it will include the specific products they need to purchase based on their dietary requirements. If a customer has selected vegan as their dietary requirement, then the products listed will only be 100% vegan, so the user will not need to read through the ingredient list to confirm this. This will save time for users that do then whenever they shop for new food items.

The next page on Mealr will present the user with the projected cost for their weekly shop depending on which store they would like to purchase their food items. The page will show all major supermarkets and food stores with the estimated price so that the user can make an informed decision on where they would like to shop. If the user would like to save money, they can shop at the store that has their items for the cheapest price, or if the customer would like to support smaller local food stores, then they can prepare what they will need to pay. This page will also present users with the option of getting the best deal if they are open to do their weekly shop at more than one store. This will work by Mealr searching for the cheapest required food items and preparing multiple shopping lists based on the supermarkets that sell them at the cheapest price. For example, if 80% of the required weekly food items are cheapest at Supermarket A, and the remaining 20% are cheapest at supermarket B, then two shopping lists will be produced. Shopping list 1 will include all items to buy at Supermarket A, and shopping list 2 will include all items to purchase as Supermarket B. It is not uncommon for more than one supermarket to be within walking distance of another supermarket or food store, so if cooking meals at the cheapest price is a high priority for the user, then they can utilise this feature to save the most money they can.

Once the users chooses where they wish to shop for their weekly groceries, the shopping list is exported to their phone’s notes application, via email, via an alternative list-making application, or via PDF. It is also hoped that Mealr can partner with supermarkets and food stores so that the shopping list can be uploaded to their supermarket accounts to allow for their groceries to be delivered straight to their door. This will eliminate the need for users to manually select the items they need to purchase one-by-one which can be a time-consuming task.

Skills Required

To develop Mealr, it would be expected that we have a solid understanding of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to build Mealr’s website and to make it interactive. As Mealr will also be developed into an Android and iOS application, it would be expected that we have working knowledge of Java and Swift programming languages to develop the application on the respective mobile operating system. Since both the website and mobile application for Mealr will need to use data from the websites of multiple different supermarkets and food stores, web scraping technology will need to be used to successfully compared the food items and their associated prices (Kenny, 2021). Web scraping is the extraction of data from a website which is then organised into a user-friendly format (Perez, 2021). Web scraping is typically done using programming languages such as Ruby or Python, along with a HTML and XML parsing library. A HTML and XML parser is software that extracts specific data from webpages and allows this data to be navigated and used in an easier to understand graphical user interface (MDN Web Docs, 2021). It would be more ideal to have a good understanding of Python so that the Beautiful Soup library can be utilised to its full potential to analyse products and their prices from multiple online webpages. Beautiful Soup is a Python library that is commonly used for web scraping (Edpresso, 2021). As Mealr will also host a database of recipes and food products, knowledge of SQL may be required so that these can be stored and added to appropriately.

Tools Required

To build the most viable product of Mealr, we will need to develop the website and corresponding mobile applications using code editors. Visual Studio Code by Microsoft will allow us to develop Mealr’s website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and will allow us to benefit from the code editors features such as prefilling closing tags and viewing features while the website is still in production. Other integrated development environments can be used when developing Mealr’s mobile applications, such as IntelliJ, XCode, and Android Studio. Another integrated development environment, PyCharm, will allow us to use web scraping libraries using Python and Beautiful Soup. GitHub will also need to be used when developing both the website and mobile applications to allow for version control when Mealr is being developed. In the emergency case of code being lost at any point in time over the development period, GitHub’s version control will allow us to recover our work from a selected period right before the code was lost. Since we will need to use a database to host the recipes and user account data, we may require Microsoft SQL server, which is a free program for developers to produce the database (Microsoft Corporation, 2019).

Funding

To build Mealr and generate revenue that maintains the application and makes a profit, funding needs to be considered. We are proposing that Mealr is free for users to use on Mealr’s website and mobile application. The free version of Mealr will give users access to three recipes each week for each dietary requirement or requirements they select. If users are wishing to have access to all ten recipes each week for all dietary requirement filters, then the user will need to pay a monthly subscription to the Mealr service which will cost $11.99 per month. Since it is a paid subscription, users will be reminded of the potential savings Mealr can provide such as advising of where to do their cheapest shop and allowing users to go to the supermarket themselves as opposed to paying delivery for weekly meal kit services such as Hello Fresh and Dinnerly. In addition to the monthly subscription service, we are hoping that supermarkets and food stores are wanting to partner with Mealr, so their products are easily viewable on the application, increasing that supermarket or food stores sales and attracting new customers for them. Mealr will charge these stores a monthly account fee which is hoping to be enough money to maintain the mobile applications, Mealr website, and pay staff working for Mealr.

Prior to this however, the team would need to apply for small business grants such as the ‘Small Business Grant VIC’ that provides small businesses with up to $30 000 of financial aid. With this grant however, it is important to have a market-ready product, fewer than 20 employees, be registered with an Australian Business Number and the product must ‘increase the overall value and volume of exports from City of Melbourne (Australian Government, 2021). An alternative to the Small Business Grant VIC is the ‘Social Enterprise Grant VIC’. This grant also provides up to $30 000 of financial aid but does not require the product to be market ready or increase exports within the City of Melbourne. Instead, the grant requires that the project will commence “activity promptly upon receiving the grant” and be financially viable (Australian Government, 2021).

Outcome

We hope that the production of Mealr will benefit many people who would like to combine the weekly task of meal planning and shopping list preparation into one. We are hoping that people who may not be able to access meal kit subscriptions can benefit from having weekly recipes to choose from no matter their dietary requirement nor where they live. We are hoping that Mealr can also save users time and headache from having to read the ingredients list of every item to make sure it’s safe or permitted to consume. It’s hoped that Mealr can partner with supermarket and food stores to create a competitive market, ultimately benefitting the consumers by their freedom of choice. ======= >>>>>>> Stashed changes
